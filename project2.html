COVID-19's Impact on the Airline Industry

This project is an interactive visualization displaying the impact of COVID-19 on the amount of daily domestic flights departing each state in the United States.

Methods

This project uses Tableau to visualize two extremely large datasets: every domestic flight across 2020, 2021, and 2022, and a daily update of COVID-19 cases in each state since the beginning of the pandemic.

Each state has a colored circle. The size of the circle corresponds with the amount of COVID-19 cases in the given day, and the color of the circle represents the number of domestic flights taking off from that state in the given day. The slider in the top right corner controls the date.

Conclusions

COVID-19 had a massive impact on the airline industry. Most states with strong democratic governors (NY) saw a drastic decrease in flights despite having similar case numbers as other states. California was a unique outlier in this case in that it had a consistent number of flights across the entire dataset. Strong Republican states like Florida and Texas saw lower impact omn flight numbers than other states. In the second wave of COVID in winter 2022, there was almost no shutdown compared to the beginning of the pandemic, despite there being far more daily cases.
